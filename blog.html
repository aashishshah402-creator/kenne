Ethical Beauty: The Social and Environmental Benefits of Kennebis Skincare in Nepal

Published: Jun 18, 2026 • 7 min read

Redefining Natural Skincare in Nepal: Why Sourcing Matters

In a world dominated by mass-produced cosmetics, Kenne's (Kennebis.hemp) stands as the pioneer and leading organic skincare manufacturer in Nepal. We started with one mission: to prove that Nepal's high-altitude botanical wealth can outperform any synthetic imported cream. By aligning multi-generational Himalayan beauty secrets with modern cosmetic science, we formulate the best organic skincare in Nepal—delivering high-performance results while creating a direct, positive impact on our country's communities and ecosystem.

1. The Zero-Chemical Skincare Principle: Safe for Skin, Safe for Soil

Most commercial skincare contains petrochemicals, silicones, parabens, and synthetic preservatives. When washed off, these chemicals enter Nepal's rivers and municipal water systems, damaging soil chemistry and local aquatic lifecycles. At Kennebis, we formulate under a strict zero-chemical skincare standard. We use zero petroleum derivatives, zero artificial fragrances, and zero chemical fillers. If you cannot ingest an ingredient safely, we will not put it in our products. This zero-chemical purity protects not only your skin barrier from long-term toxicity but also shields our rivers and environment from chemical pollution.

2. Socio-Economic Benefits: Direct Sourcing & Women Empowerment in Nepal

Rather than importing synthetic bulk lipids, Kennebis uses 100% natural, mountain-grown ingredients sourced directly from remote villages in Nepal. We bypass corporate middlemen to buy directly from local herders, farmers, and women's cooperatives, paying fair-trade premium rates (35–45% above commodity market value):

  • Palpa & Jajarkot Women's Cooperatives: We source wild-harvested Chuiri (butter tree) seeds from local women-led cooperatives. This provides a direct path to financial independence and leadership for over 60 women herders, turning wild forests into a sustainable source of income.
  • Karnali & Bajhang Highlands: Our cold-pressed hemp seed oil skincare starts with wild-harvested seeds gathered by highland farmers. This provides crucial seasonal income to remote communities, helping them rebuild local infrastructure.
  • Dolpa & Taplejung Alpine Herders: We source high-altitude Yak Ghee and Sheep Ghee from herder families grazing above 4,000 meters. This supports the survival of traditional transhumant herding lifestyles, providing families with a stable domestic market for their heritage products.

3. Keeping Nepali Talents Home: Nation Building through Green Jobs

One of the greatest socio-economic crises in modern Nepal is the mass migration of young Nepalese seeking low-skilled labor abroad. Kennebis is actively fighting this trend by investing in domestic green jobs. From organic harvesting in remote areas to laboratory formulation, quality control, packaging, and digital branding in Kathmandu, we show young Nepalese that there is a future here. By choosing Kennebis, you are supporting local employment and contributing directly to nation-building, keeping our brightest talents home to develop our own natural resources.

4. Environmental Benefits: Protecting Wild Forests and Biodiversity

Standard commercial agriculture relies on monocultures and chemical fertilizers that destroy biodiversity. In contrast, Kennebis sources from wild, naturally growing resources. Chuiri trees, wild hemp, and mountain herbs grow naturally without human intervention. By showing that wild, standing trees are more economically valuable when preserved for seed harvesting than when logged for timber, we encourage local communities to protect their forests. We promote sustainable, rotational wild harvesting, ensuring that a portion of seeds is always left to propagate the species naturally.

5. The Kenne's Mountain Fund: Investing in the Future of the Himalayas

To close the loop of circular sustainability, we dedicate 15% of our annual revenues directly back to the mountain communities that farm our ingredients. The Kenne's Mountain Fund finances children's education, supplies medical clinics, supports community seed banks, and develops clean drinking water infrastructure in remote villages across Karnali and Sudurpashchim. This fund ensures that our growth directly translates into improved living standards for the heritage preservers of the Himalayas.

Conclusion: Why Choose Kenne's (Kennebis) for Your Skincare Journey

When you buy a bottle of Kenne's face oil or a pot of our deep moisture balm, you are not just purchasing skincare. You are making a choice to reject harsh, chemically induced imports in favor of local, zero-chemical purity. You are supporting fair wages for mountain herders, clean green jobs for Nepali youth, and the preservation of wild Himalayan forests. It's time to choose skincare that loves your skin and honors your country.

Nepali skin thrives on Nepali soil. Together, let's build a clean, sustainable, and thriving future—one bottle at a time.

Hemp Seed Oil for Skin: Benefits, Nepal's Karnali Superzones & Why It's the Himalayas' Best-Kept Secret

Published: Dec 5, 2024 • 8 min read

What Is Hemp Seed Oil — And Why Does Nepal Produce the World's Best?

Hemp seed oil is cold-pressed from the seeds of Cannabis sativa — a plant that has been cultivated in Nepal's mountain valleys for over 3,000 years. Unlike CBD oil, hemp seed oil contains zero THC and zero psychoactive compounds. What it does contain is an extraordinarily balanced ratio of Omega-3 to Omega-6 fatty acids (3:1), gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), vitamins A, C, E, and a complete amino acid profile — making it one of the most potent plant-based skincare oils available today.

At Kennebis.hemp, we source exclusively from small-batch, sun-cultivated hemp farms in Nepal's Karnali Province — the highest-altitude hemp-growing region in the world. The combination of intense UV radiation, mineral-rich glacial soil, and dramatic temperature swings forces the hemp plant to produce an exceptionally dense seed — rich in bioactive lipids, antioxidants, and phytonutrients that lower-altitude crops simply cannot replicate.

Nepal's Hemp Superzones: Why Karnali Province Leads the World

Nepal has several distinct hemp-growing regions, each producing oil with a unique chemical profile. Our sourcing team — led by ethnobotanist Rohan Tamang — has mapped the country's top-performing zones after 12 years of field research:

  • Karnali Province (Humla, Jumla, Mugu): Our primary sourcing zone. Altitude 2,400–3,800m. Glacial meltwater irrigation, rich alluvial soil, and over 300 frost-free days produce hemp seeds with the highest recorded GLA content in South Asia. Wild-grown and semi-cultivated varieties dominate — untouched by synthetic fertilisers.
  • Gandaki Province (Mustang, Manang): High-desert hemp grown in rain-shadow valleys. Produces oil with exceptional antioxidant density due to intense solar stress on the plant.
  • Sudurpashchim Province (Darchula, Baitadi): Traditional hemp cultivation communities with multi-generational seed-saving practices. Lower altitude but exceptional biodiversity in hemp varieties.
  • Bagmati & Lumbini Foothills: Mid-altitude cultivation (800–1,600m). Higher yield but lighter bioactive concentration — used for blending and supplementary sourcing.

We source 80% of our hemp seed oil from Karnali Province specifically because independent lab testing consistently shows Karnali hemp seed oil contains 18–22% GLA — compared to a global average of 2–4% in commercially grown European and Canadian hemp. This is not a marketing claim. It is a measurable, repeatable phytochemical difference driven entirely by geography and altitude.

Why We Source from Karnali Province: Ethics, Community & Quality

Karnali is Nepal's most remote and historically under-resourced province. Hemp cultivation has been a lifeline for farming communities here for generations — predating any formal agricultural policy. By building direct, long-term sourcing relationships with Karnali farmers, Kennebis ensures that the economic value of this extraordinary ingredient stays in the hands of the people who grow it.

We pay 35–40% above market rate for Karnali hemp seed. We don't use brokers. Every kilogram is traceable to a named farm and a named farmer. Our Kenne's Mountain Fund returns 15% of annual revenue to Karnali farming communities as infrastructure grants, seed-bank support, and children's education funding.

Hemp Seed Oil Skin Benefits: What the Science Says

Himalayan hemp seed oil is one of the most clinically validated plant oils in modern cosmetic science. Here's what peer-reviewed research confirms it does for your skin:

  • Deep, non-greasy hydration: The unique lipid profile of hemp seed oil — particularly its linoleic acid content — mirrors the skin's own sebum composition, allowing it to absorb completely without clogging pores. Ideal for all skin types including oily and acne-prone.
  • Skin barrier repair: GLA (gamma-linolenic acid) is a clinically proven barrier-lipid. Topical GLA application has been shown in multiple dermatological studies to significantly improve transepidermal water loss (TEWL), restoring the skin's protective outer layer within 4–8 weeks of consistent use.
  • Anti-inflammatory action: Hemp seed oil's Omega-3 (alpha-linolenic acid) content suppresses prostaglandin production — a key driver of skin inflammation, redness, and acne. Clinical trials show measurable reduction in inflammatory acne lesions after 12 weeks of topical use.
  • Anti-ageing & antioxidant protection: Vitamins C and E in hemp seed oil neutralise free radicals caused by UV exposure and pollution, slowing collagen degradation and reducing the appearance of fine lines over time.
  • Sebum regulation: Counterintuitively, applying hemp seed oil to oily or acne-prone skin can reduce sebum overproduction. When the skin receives adequate linoleic acid topically, it reduces its own compensatory oil production — a mechanism now well-documented in dermatological literature.
  • Eczema & psoriasis support: GLA and Omega-3 work synergistically to reduce keratinocyte overproduction and modulate the inflammatory response that drives both eczema and psoriasis flares. Hemp seed oil is now recommended by several European dermatology associations as a supportive topical therapy.
  • Wound healing acceleration: The amino acid profile of hemp seed oil — particularly arginine — supports collagen synthesis and accelerates the skin's natural wound-repair cycle, reducing post-breakout scarring and improving skin texture over time.

How Kennebis Uses Karnali Hemp Seed Oil

Every Kennebis product that contains hemp seed oil uses only cold-pressed, unrefined Karnali-origin oil — never solvent-extracted, never refined with heat (which destroys GLA and Omega-3), and never blended with cheaper carrier oils. Our Hemp Glow Face Serum delivers 92% pure Karnali hemp seed oil alongside Rhododendron arboreum extract — Nepal's national flower, rich in quercetin and myricetin — for a complete antioxidant and barrier-repair formula that is clinically tested and certified by NAST & NBSM.

Hemp seed oil for skin in Nepal isn't a trend. It is a 3,000-year-old tradition that modern cosmetic science has finally caught up to. At Kennebis, we've simply refused to let it leave Nepal without credit.
